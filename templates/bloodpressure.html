What is Blood Pressure?

Blood pressure is the force exerted by blood against the walls of the arteries. High blood pressure can damage the heart, brain, kidneys, and blood vessels.

Types of Blood Pressure

  • Normal Blood Pressure – Healthy blood pressure level.
  • Elevated Blood Pressure – Higher than normal but not hypertension.
  • Stage 1 Hypertension – Mild high blood pressure.
  • Stage 2 Hypertension – Severe high blood pressure.
  • Hypertensive Crisis – Medical emergency.

Blood Pressure Levels

Category Blood Pressure (mmHg)
Normal Below 120/80
Elevated 120–129 / Below 80
Stage 1 Hypertension 130–139 / 80–89
Stage 2 Hypertension 140/90 or Above
Hypertensive Crisis 180/120 or Above

Danger Levels

⚠️ Elevated Blood Pressure: 120–129 / Below 80 mmHg
🚨 High Blood Pressure: Above 140/90 mmHg
🚨 Hypertensive Crisis: Above 180/120 mmHg

Symptoms

  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Chest pain (severe cases)

Prevention

  • Reduce salt intake
  • Exercise regularly
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Avoid smoking
  • Manage stress
  • Get adequate sleep

  2. Reduce salt intake.

Emergency Warning Signs

🚨 Severe chest pain
🚨 Difficulty breathing
🚨 Sudden weakness or numbness

🚨 Loss of consciousness
